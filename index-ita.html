Cool places you can visit with us

Brijuni

Brijuni are islands at the south coast of Istrian peninsula. It consists of 14 smaller islands and all of them belong to the National Park.
On the island of Veli Brijun which is open for the visitors, there is a marina for yachts, an excellent restaurant, and the residence of the former Yugoslavian president Josip Broz Tito. As well as his personal car, Museums and many other interesting points, as beautifull sandy beaches.

Rovinj

Along with Dubrovnik, Rovinj is the most visited town on Croatian coast. This charming old Mediterranean city is located on a kind of peninsula. It has a magnificent view on the sea from north, west and south. Provides fantastic view of the surrounding islands.
Its historical stone streets, the Church of St. Euphemia, the excellent gastronomic offer of Istria with truffels, prosciutto, wine, cheese, olive oil, sea food specialities, have left visitors breathless for decades.

Poreč

Poreč is also a midleage Mediterranean town on the Adriatic coast. Only 20 minutes by boat from Umag. It offers a large selection of beaches, islands, restaurants and historical sight such as Euphrasian Basillic(UNESCO).

Pula

Pula is located 20 minutes south from Brijuni islands. It is best known for the Colosseum, which dates back to the Roman Empire. It is the only preserved example of an ancient Arena along with Colosseum in Rome. There are also city gates from Roman Empire and the Temple of roman emperor Octavian August, as well as many museums, restaurants, squares and parks.

Lim fjord

Lim fjord is located between Vrsar and Rovinj. It is 7 kilometers long. Serves as shellfish farm and touristic attraction. There are moorings for tourist boats, restaurants, caffe bars and souvenir shops.
